Reference Text Book: Olivier Blanchard, et.al. Macroeconomics: An European Perspective (As a reference, Not Required)

All required contents will be provided through lecture notes absolutely free.

Description: This course is considered to be intermediate level macroeconomic course for undergraduate students. The fundamental models and ideas in modern macroeconomic theory will be introduced. Some basic tools to collect and analyze macroeconomic data will be introduced to catch up with the job market demand in big data era.

Objectives: At the end of this course, students are expected be able to use basic macroeconomic ideas and data analysis tools to provide solid economic analysis for contemporary economic issues.

Prerequisites: Econ 203: Principle of Economics (Macro) with minimum grade of C. No previous GitHub, R or Python experience required, I’ll give you step-by-step tutoring if we use some of them in sometime.

Tentative Course Outline

  • Introduction

    • Pandemic Lockdown, Government Relief Package, Gig Workers and Non-Economic Forces

  • The Short Run

    • The Goods Market

    • Financial Markets

    • Goods and Financial Markets: The IS-LM Model

    • The IS-LM Model in an Open Economy

  • The Medium Run

    • The Labor Market

    • Putting All Markets Together: The AS-AD Model

    • The Natural Rate of Unemployment and the Philips Curve

    • Inflation, Activity and Nominal Money Growth

  • The Long Run

    • The Facts of Growth

    • Saving, Capital Accumulation and Output

    • Technological Progress and Growth
